# About


<div style="text-align: justify">

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Government at the University of Essex. My supervisors are <span style="color:#323434"> **Professor Royce Carroll**</span>, <span style="color:#323434"> **Dr Nicole Baerg**</span> and <span style="color:#323434"> **Dr Akitaka Matsuo**</span>. My doctoral research focuses on Taiwan legislative politics (and beyond). I am interested in distributive politics and roll call polarization within congress, and particularly in how electoral motives shape preference, representation and ideological positionings. The work brings computational techniques and field experiments to legislative studies. I also contributed and maintain [asmcjr](https://uniofessex.github.io/asmcjr/) package on Github. Prior to my PhD, I worked for four years as a Research Assistant in the Institute of Political Science at Academia Sinica, Taiwan. You can find my latest 
curriculum vitae from [here](https://raw.githack.com/yl17124/CV/master/CV.pdf). 


My PhD research project is funded by the Ministry of Education (July 2017 - June 2021) and the Council of Indigenous Peoples in Taiwan. If you're interested in researches related to Taiwan, cross-strait relations, Asia-Pacific region and would like to apply for fellowship or scholarship from Taiwan, please don't hesitate to reach out to me.
